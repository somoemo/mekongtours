As the largest city in Vietnam, Saigon is a bustling metropolis filled with rich history, vibrant culture, and delicious food. But what many travelers may not know is that just a short drive away from the city lies some of the most beautiful beaches in the country. So if you're looking for a quick escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, why not head to one of these stunning beaches near Saigon?

Vung Tau Beach

Located just 125 kilometers southeast of Saigon, Vung Tau Beach is a popular destination for both locals and tourists alike. This beach town offers a perfect blend of relaxation and adventure with its pristine sandy beaches and exciting water activities.

One of the main attractions of Vung Tau Beach is its long stretch of golden sand, making it an ideal spot for sunbathing and building sandcastles. The calm and clear waters are also perfect for swimming and other water sports such as jet skiing and parasailing. And for those seeking a more adrenaline-pumping experience, there are also options for scuba diving and snorkeling.



Aside from the beach, Vung Tau also offers a variety of cultural and historical sites to explore. The most popular one being the Christ of Vung Tau, a 32-meter tall statue of Jesus Christ that sits atop of Nui Nho Mountain. Visitors can take a cable car ride to the top and enjoy stunning views of the city and the beach.

Long Hai Beach

If you're looking for a quieter and more secluded beach experience, then Long Hai Beach is the perfect destination for you. Located about 100 kilometers southeast of Saigon, this hidden gem boasts crystal clear waters and a peaceful atmosphere.

Long Hai Beach is still relatively untouched by mass tourism, making it a great escape from the crowds and noise of the city. The beach is lined with small resorts and bungalows, providing a peaceful and relaxing setting for your stay. And with its calm waters, it's also a great spot for swimming and snorkeling.

Another must-see attraction in Long Hai is the Minh Dam Mountain and Temple. This mountain offers a challenging but rewarding hike and at the top, visitors are treated to breathtaking views of the beach and the surrounding area. The temple, which dates back to the 18th century, is also a popular spot for locals to pray and make offerings.

Mui Ne Beach

Known as the "Hawaii of Vietnam," Mui Ne Beach is a must-visit for any beach lover. Located about 200 kilometers northeast of Saigon, this beach town is famous for its stunning landscape, strong winds, and perfect conditions for water sports.

One of the main attractions of Mui Ne Beach is its sand dunes. The Red Sand Dunes and White Sand Dunes offer a unique and picturesque landscape, perfect for taking photos and trying out sandboarding. And for those looking for a more relaxing activity, there are also plenty of options for spa treatments and yoga classes along the beach.

But the highlight of Mui Ne Beach is its strong winds, making it a haven for water sports enthusiasts. The beach is famous for windsurfing and kitesurfing, with many schools and rental shops offering lessons and equipment. And during the peak season, visitors can also witness the annual International Kite Festival, where colorful kites take over the sky.

Con Dao Island

If you're willing to travel a bit further from Saigon, then a visit to Con Dao Island is definitely worth it. Located about 230 kilometers southeast of Saigon, this remote island offers a unique and untouched beach experience.

Con Dao Island is made up of 16 small islands and boasts some of the most beautiful beaches in Vietnam. The most popular one being Dam Trau Beach, with its crystal clear waters and white sandy shores. Visitors can also take a boat trip to the nearby islands to explore hidden beaches and snorkel in the pristine waters.

Aside from its beaches, Con Dao Island is also rich in history and culture. The Con Dao National Park is a popular spot for hiking and birdwatching, and also holds a dark past as a former prison during the Vietnam War. The Con Dao Prison Museum offers a glimpse into the island's history and the struggles of its prisoners.

Phu Quoc Island

Last but definitely not least, is Phu Quoc Island, known as the "Pearl Island" of Vietnam. Located about 300 kilometers southwest of Saigon, this island paradise boasts some of the most beautiful and unspoiled beaches in the country.

With its warm turquoise waters and white sandy beaches, Phu Quoc Island is a dream destination for beach lovers. Long Beach is the most popular beach on the island, offering a wide range of water activities such as snorkeling, kayaking, and jet skiing. And for those looking for a more serene experience, there are also plenty of secluded beaches to explore.

Aside from its beaches, Phu Quoc Island is also famous for its seafood. Visitors can indulge in fresh and delicious seafood dishes at the many restaurants and markets around the island. And for those interested in learning more about the local culture, there are also options for visiting pearl farms and fish sauce factories.
